About the VL2 Student Network

Dr. Laura-Ann Petitto, Science Director, Co-PI
Dr. Tom Allen, Co-PI

The VL2 Student Network is made up of undergraduate, graduate, and doctoral students from Gallaudet University and its partnering institutions, all who are interested in gaining a greater understanding of the biological, linguistic, cognitive, socio-cultural, and pedagogical conditions that influence the acquisition of language and knowledge through the visual modality in order to promote optimal practices in education.  Our vision fosters the promotion of eager and sharp young minds to explore, learn, and share the linguistic biological, neurological, socio-cultural, pedagogical, and cognitive elements that exist in a visual world seen through the eyes of deaf, hard-of-hearing, and hearing scholars.  By participating in an interdisciplinary science, VL2 students gain formidable research, grant-writing, ethics, and networking skills that position them well for outstanding academic and educational careers, thereby transforming how society views deaf people, reading acquisition in deaf children, and the very foundations of language, literacy, and cognition itself.

Membership in the VL2 Student Network grants scholars access to a wealth of resources, including:
• Weekly VL2 Student Network Lecture Series presentations from the world's top researchers
• Travel to yearly VL2 Student Network retreats
• Regular updates about conferences, lectures, and job postings through our email listserv and the VL2 Student Network Facebook page
• Mentoring and networking opportunities with students, junior and senior research scientists, and experts in education and research translation
• Grant writing, grant reviewing, and leadership experience
• Funds for travel and research (with restrictions)
* A rich ASL/English, bilingual research environment (sign language fluency is not required, but is often acquired by being part of VL2!)

Student Review Committee (SRC) Funding

The Student Review Committee (SRC) provides financial support for VL2 Student Network Members to attend professional and academic workshops, meetings, or conferences in which they may present their own work and/or learn more about relevant issues in their field of work.  It also provides support for students who wish to gain acdded training in research methodologies or technology (i.e., but visiting a different laboratory or undertaking a specialized skills course).  Lastly, the SRC supports students wishing to initiate, conduct, and/or complete their research projects, in which they will distribute their findings through publications or conferences.  In short, the SRC funds these types of activities:
• Travel to workshops, meetings, or conferences
• Training in new research methodologies
• Initiating or continuing research projects

To see the complete SRC guidelines for 2016–2017, click here.

Questions about SRC funding should be directed to the SRC Chair of the Student Leadership Team (SLT).  You can find the information for the current SRC Chair on our contact page.

Maximum monetary allotments are established at the beginning of each academic year and my change from time to time.  Therefore, it is important that you review the complete SRC guidelines for the academic year in which you are interested in applying.  Successful grant applications could be awarded:
• Research funds for computer equipment and participants to complete a dissertation-level study
• Funding for travel, hotel, and conference registration for a meeting or conference relative to your field of work
• Funding for travel and per diem to a VL2-affiliated laboratory to gain experience in a specific line of work
• Tuition costs for an online course in a specific topic not offerred by your university

Become a VL2 Student Network Scholar!

Students at VL2-affiliated universities may apply for VL2 Student Network membership.  Partnerships are created when Gallaudet University and representatives from the partner university have signed a Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) or a letter of support.  Current partnerships include:

• American University
* Gallaudet University        
• George Washington University        
• Georgetown University        
• Georgia Institute of Technology
• Indiana University, Bloomington
• Lamar University
• Rochester Institute of Technology
• San Diego State University
* Stanford University
• University of California, San Diego
• University of Connecticuit
* University of Hong Kong
• University of Illinois, Urbana-Champaign
* University of Manitoba
• University of New Mexico
• University of Texas at Austin
* University of Tulsa
• Vanderbilt University
• Yale University

If your university is not on this list, please contact Dr. Ilaria Berteletti, our VL2 Student Network Faculty Liaison, to inquire about partner opportunities.  In addition, there are several universities with which an MoU is currently pending: California State University, Fresno, University College London, University of Alberta, and University of Maryland.

Membership is free.  In order to become a member, you must complete an application.  In the past, we accepted applications during an open enrollment period.  We have since phased this out and now accept applications on a rolling basis.  Your application will be reviewed by a committee, and you will be notified of their decision within one to two weeks of receiving all of your materials.  We do not have to have an MoU with your university in order for you to submit an application.  We will work with each applicant on a case-by-case basis to develop a partnership with your university.  Upon the approval of your application and the establishment of an MoU with your institution, you will be granted membership.  To maintain membership, you must be continually enrolled as a student at a VL2-partnered university, and you may be asked to complete an annual progress report.  Membership is valid until your graduate date.
